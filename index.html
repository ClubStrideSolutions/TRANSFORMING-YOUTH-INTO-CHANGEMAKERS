üîç The Five Pressure Systems: Youth Research That Changes Everything

Through comprehensive community research, youth researchers discovered that Vallejo's challenges aren't isolated issues but interconnected systems that reinforce each other:

1

The Hunger-Stress-Risk Connection

  • 75% food insecurity (vs. 48% CA average)
  • Food-insecure youth: 1.5-1.6x higher STI risk
  • Direct link: hunger ‚Üí anxiety ‚Üí academics
2

Digital Divide as Health Barrier

  • 42% rate internet as "terrible"
  • Affects homework, college apps, telehealth
  • Creates cascading disadvantages
3

Paperwork Paralysis

  • SSN confusion blocks FAFSA
  • Complex forms prevent service access
  • Self-reinforcing avoidance patterns
4

Transportation Trap

  • 46% cite transport as #1 barrier
  • 15,000+ residents >1 mile from groceries
  • Compounds health/education/employment
5

Stigma & Safety Spiral

  • 80% feel unsafe in shelters
  • Only 1 in 5 teens get STI testing
  • 91% prefer trauma-informed spaces

üí° Youth-Designed Solutions: Breaking the Cycles

Leverage Moves (High-Impact Interventions)

  • Service Bundling at Intersection Points

    Pop-up clinics at food banks offering: STI testing + CalFresh signup + WiFi hotspots. Addresses Systems 1, 2, and 3 simultaneously.

  • Peer Bureaucracy Navigators

    Youth trained to help peers with FAFSA, shelter intake, benefits. Breaks System 3 while building agency. 42% of graduates volunteering.

  • Mobile Service Delivery

    Bring services to youth vs. requiring transportation. Directly disrupts System 4.

  • Stigma-Free Zones

    Youth-designed safe spaces with peer support. "It's okay to not be okay" messaging.
